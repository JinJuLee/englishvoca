adjective

ubiquitous

/juːˈbɪk.wɪ.təs/

도처에 있는, 어디에나 존재하는

In daily American use

Casual yet faintly formal — reached for when something feels so widespread it is inescapable: "Avocado toast became ubiquitous around 2015." Carries a small trace of mild exasperation; rarely neutral.

In TOEFL reading

Common in academic passages on technology adoption, species distribution, and cultural phenomena. Synonyms tested in vocabulary questions: omnipresent, pervasive, prevalent, widespread.

"Smartphones have become ubiquitous in modern society — they shape not only how we communicate, but how we remember."
